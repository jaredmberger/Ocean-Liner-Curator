Selected Enthusiast & Collector Sites

A curated list of ocean liner enthusiast sites, collector references, societies, and community resources. These are included because they are useful, distinctive, or well known within the wider liner-interest world.

This page is meant as a companion to Ocean Liner Curator rather than a substitute for it. Some of the sites below are research-oriented, some are society-based, and some reflect collecting culture more than formal documentation. Their value lies in perspective, imagery, ephemera, enthusiasm, and specialist focus.

⁂ Inclusion here reflects usefulness and relevance, not blanket endorsement of every claim or interpretation. As always, when a detail matters, trace it back as far as possible and compare it against surviving evidence.

Enthusiast & Research Sites

Independent sites created by liner enthusiasts, collectors, and researchers with strong topical focus.

  • Mark Chirnside’s Reception Room A particularly valuable independent research site focused on major liners including Olympic, Titanic, and related White Star subjects. Especially useful for detailed historical articles, close documentary work, and carefully argued reconsiderations of well-known claims and assumptions.
  • Cabin Liners A particularly strong enthusiast resource on ocean travel in the 1920s and 1930s, built around brochures, deck plans, photographs, menus, and other memorabilia. Especially useful for interwar interiors, travel culture, and cabin-class context.
  • The Last Ocean Liners A focused reference site on the last great liner era, with 1960s schedules, route groupings, and ship comparisons by size, speed, year built, and related measures. Very useful for late-period service context.
  • The Ocean Liner Enthusiasts A long-running enthusiast hub built around bringing the ocean liner community together. Best approached as a community-centered reference point with a strong fan-history atmosphere.

Collector & Memorabilia References

Sites valuable for artifact culture, collecting standards, surviving material, and the visual world of liners beyond the ship itself.

  • Luxury Liner Row A collector-focused site centered on high-end ocean liner memorabilia, interiors, and presentation pieces. Particularly useful for visual reference and the aesthetic language of luxury liners, including furniture, fittings, and curated artifacts associated with first-class travel.
  • The Pursers Locker A longstanding maritime antiques and collecting site centered on original ocean liner and royal-yacht material. Particularly useful for seeing high-grade surviving objects and the material culture surrounding famous liners.

Societies & Enthusiast Communities

Membership groups, societies, and broad ship-interest communities that can widen the conversation beyond a single site or collector.

  • Ocean Liner Society A non-profit society whose members celebrate passenger ships in many forms, from historic liners to later passenger vessels. A strong addition if you want one established society link on the page.
  • Cunard Steamship Society A specialist society for readers interested in Cunard history and tradition. More line-specific than generalist, but useful for deeper Cunard-focused material.
  • Passenger Ship Enthusiast Association A broad passenger-ship society for enthusiasts interested in life and travel at sea. Best suited as a community link rather than a formal research destination.
  • Ships Nostalgia A large general ship-enthusiast forum and discussion community. Broader than ocean liners alone, but useful for photographs, shared memories, and enthusiast leads that may point toward harder-to-find material.

Suggested Use

These sites work best in combination. Independent enthusiast pages can supply atmosphere, photos, brochures, and specialized focus; collector sites can sharpen one’s eye for surviving material culture; societies and forums can surface leads, discussion, and lesser-known references. The strongest approach is to use them as adjacent tools while keeping final interpretation evidence-first.
