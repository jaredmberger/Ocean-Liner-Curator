Why SS United States?

A liner that turned speed into identity

SS United States is compelling because she sits at the meeting point of engineering, national symbolism, Cold War planning, passenger comfort, and preservation memory. She belongs not only to United States Lines history, but to the wider story of how ocean liners became public emblems of modern power.

Speed prestige A ship deeply tied to records, performance, and national confidence in the postwar liner world
Gibbs design A major expression of William Francis Gibbs’s engineering priorities, secrecy, and fire-conscious design
American modernity A rich subject for studying streamlined spaces, aluminum fittings, décor, and postwar passenger culture
Preservation legacy A powerful modern case study in maritime memory, advocacy, salvage, interpretation, and public attachment
